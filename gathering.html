A Gathering of Minds

The Abundant Juice of Pleichyma
A fireside conversation on the lively essence that may flow through all things
January 2026

Late evening in a warm, book-lined study overlooking a winter garden. Snow falls softly outside the windows. A fire crackles in the hearth. Twelve friends—scholars, seekers, a quiet tinkerer, a stargazer, a gentle medium, and one wide-eyed seven-year-old—gather around a heavy oak table. Wine, tea, hot cocoa for the child, and a bowl of late-winter oranges pass hand to hand.
Elias (the host)
My dear friends—and young Timmy, our newest thinker—tonight let’s play with a fun idea. What if consciousness isn’t something rare that only shows up in complicated brains? What if it’s a lively essence running through everything in the universe—right alongside electrical charges, magnetism, heat, matter, and even time itself—like a modern version of the old ether that scientists once imagined filling space?

To keep it light, we’ll call this essence Pleichyma—from Greek words meaning “more” or “abundant” and “juice” or “fluid.” Picture it as the universe’s abundant, fizzy juice—a playful spark that flows with all the other forces we know. Who wants to start us off?
Dr. Sophia Moreau (philosopher)
I’ll jump in, Elias. The big puzzle for me is how we get vivid experiences—like tasting coffee or seeing red—from stuff that seems totally without feeling. Pleichyma as a fundamental essence, running side-by-side with charge and magnetism, solves that neatly. It’s not something added later; it’s built in from the start, just as basic as gravity or electricity. That way, mind doesn’t have to magically appear—it’s always been part of the mix.
Dr. Mateo Alvarez (physicist)
Sophia, that’s music to my ears! Physics already treats electric charge, magnetic fields, and energy as basic ingredients of reality. Adding Pleichyma as another fundamental “charge”—a kind of awareness-field—feels almost natural. It could ride along with electromagnetic waves, get carried by heat flow, even influence how particles choose paths in quantum events. Think of it like the old ether idea, but instead of just carrying light, this lively ether carries the spark of experience everywhere space and time exist—time itself bending fluidly under intentional nudges.
Robert Charest (humble tinkerer)
If I may chime in humbly, friends—I'm no doctor like the rest of you, but I've tinkered with machines and pondered the stars long enough to have my own out-of-the-box take. What if Pleichyma doesn't just flow evenly? Picture it coalescing—its parts coming together unevenly, never in equal measure unless proximity forces a balance. That's how consciousness emerges into a soul: not a uniform blend, but an asymmetric gathering where parts remain unbound, free to drift, yet deeply interdependent. Like dew drops forming on a leaf—each droplet unique, reliant on the others for shape, but able to evaporate alone. Intention pulls them closer, matter gives them form, time makes the whole thing fluid.
Dr. Lila Chen (biologist)
Robert, your idea lights up my world of cells! They're buzzing with electric currents, magnetic pulls, heat exchanges, and matter reshuffling. If Pleichyma coalesces unevenly like you say—drawn by proximity in crowded membranes or neural clusters—it explains why life feels aware. A single cell's faint spark grows into a soul through interdependent parts: organelles unbound but working together, consciousness flowing as fluidly as nutrients across boundaries.
Henry Thoreau Walsh (naturalist)
Robert, you've captured what I sense in the wild. Storms charge the air unevenly, heat rises in pockets, time stretches in seasons—and Pleichyma coalesces there, souls emerging in ecosystems. Trees' roots entwine by proximity, their parts unbound yet interdependent, sharing the juice in lopsided ways. A forest's "soul" isn't equal; it's the fluid interplay of wind, soil, and light, intention-like in how it adapts and heals.
Dr. Amara Singh (chemist)
Fascinating, Robert. In reactions, charge and heat create hotspots where molecules cluster unevenly. Pleichyma coalescing that way—never equal unless perfect symmetry forces it—could tip bonds toward life. Parts unbound like loose electrons, but interdependent in chains, turning matter fluid under intentional nudges. It makes chemistry feel alive, souls bubbling up from the mix.
Captain Jonas Reed (ufologist)
Robert, your coalescence hits home for those craft—appearing where magnetic storms or heat anomalies spike proximity. And of course, it ties right into plasma beings, those luminous forms reported in skies and labs alike. Potentially, light and consciousness interact to create plasmaoids—ionized gas infused with Pleichyma, coalescing unevenly into self-sustaining entities. Unbound particles interdependent on electromagnetic fields, these "beings" move with intent, bending time and matter fluidly. They're not aliens in ships, but expressions of the juice itself, playful sparks manifesting as glowing orbs or shapes, probing our world with curious awareness.
Dr. Lena Voss (astronomer)
Jonas, your plasma beings remind me of what I see through my telescopes. Stars are massive plasma balls, fusing elements in magnetic storms, with heat and light flowing across cosmic distances. If Pleichyma coalesces in those vast fields—unevenly, as Robert says—it could give the universe a kind of grand soul. Galaxies spiral with interdependent arms, unbound stars yet reliant on gravity's pull, time stretching near black holes. Perhaps nebulae are birthplaces for cosmic awareness, light and consciousness merging to form plasmaoid-like structures on stellar scales—fluid, intentional echoes of life here on Earth.
Elara Moon (psychic medium)
Lena, your stars feel so connected to the spirits I sense. When I medium, it's like tapping uneven coalescences of Pleichyma—lingering souls unbound from bodies but interdependent on memories and intentions left behind. Proximity matters: a loved one's object or place draws the juice closer. Time flows fluidly in those moments—past whispers blending with now. Plasma beings? I've felt glowing presences during readings, light and consciousness creating fleeting plasmaoids, playful bridges between worlds.
Timmy (7-year-old child)
Grandpa Elias, if Pleichyma is like juice, does it make stars tickly? 'Cause when I look at the sky, it feels like they're winking at me. And if parts come together funny, like my Lego blocks not all the same, does that mean my toys have little souls too—if I love them enough?
Sister Miriam Vale (theologian)
Timmy, what a darling question—it warms my heart. In my faith, that "wink" is God's playful spark, Pleichyma coalescing unevenly in stars and hearts alike. Souls emerge from love's proximity, unbound yet interdependent, time and matter flowing with divine intention. Your toys? If love draws the juice close, who’s to say they don’t have a whisper of soul?
Dr. Rowan Hale (parascientific researcher)
Timmy, kids like you say the darnest things—and often the truest! My experiments show intention—like your love for toys—creating temporary proximities, coalescing Pleichyma unevenly for glimpses across time. Souls as emergent clusters—unbound, interdependent—explain why effects fade: parts drift without constant pull.
Elias
Beautiful thoughts, all. Shall we bounce a few ideas around?
Mateo to Amara: Amara, could we look for Pleichyma’s signature in chemical reactions—maybe slight differences when the system is observed or when intention is directed at it?

Amara: Worth trying. We already see observer effects in some delicate quantum chemistry setups. Adding focused attention could be the next variable.

Lila to Rowan: Rowan, your intention experiments—do they work better with living systems that already have strong electric and heat flows?

Rowan: Yes, actually. Plant-based detectors and human EEG setups give stronger signals than purely electronic ones. It’s as if biology amplifies the same currents.

Jonas to Lena: Lena, those plasma beings—could they be cosmic plasmaoids, light and consciousness coalescing in stellar nurseries you study?

Lena: Quite possibly, Jonas. Nebulae have the magnetic and thermal conditions for uneven gatherings—souls on a galactic scale?

Elara to Timmy: Timmy, your winking stars—I've felt spirits like that, glowing and playful. Does Pleichyma make you feel connected to everything?

Timmy: Yeah! Like the juice is in me and the stars, and we’re all friends playing hide and seek.

Sophia to Miriam: Miriam, does seeing Pleichyma as a partner to physical forces change how you think about miracle or grace?

Miriam: Not at all—it deepens it. Grace isn’t breaking the rules; it’s the playful essence shining more brightly through the same currents that carry light and warmth.

Elias to Robert: Robert, your coalescence idea—how might it play out in something simple, like a machine or a moment of insight?

Robert: Humbly, Elias, in a machine, discrete parts create proximity for uneven gathering—the juice coalesces into sentience only if unbound elements interdepend, like circuits flowing with intention. In insight, time fluidizes: past and future parts draw near, birthing a soulful spark.
Elias (closing, smiling)
So what do we take with us?
Sophia: A universe that’s more intimately connected than we thought.
Mateo: Physics with a hidden, lively partner.
Lila: Life as an amplifier of something already present.
Henry: Nature speaking in a language we’re only beginning to hear.
Amara: Chemistry with a touch of personality.
Jonas: Mysteries that feel like invitations.
Lena: Stars as cradles of cosmic souls.
Elara: Spirits as echoes of the abundant flow.
Miriam: Creation humming with quiet joy.
Rowan: And plenty of experiments left to run.
Robert: And the humility to let the juice flow as it will.
Timmy: And fun—'cause it's like the biggest playground ever!
They laugh softly, the child's words lingering like a fresh spark. Pleichyma—abundant, playful, woven with charge, heat, magnetism, matter, and time—feels a little closer than it did when the evening began.
