A Gathering of Minds

The Abundant Juice of Pleichyma
A fireside conversation on the lively essence that may flow through all things
January 2026

Late evening in a warm, book-lined study overlooking a winter garden. Snow falls softly outside the windows. A fire crackles in the hearth. Twelve friends—scholars, seekers, a quiet tinkerer, a stargazer, a gentle medium, and one wide-eyed seven-year-old—gather around a heavy oak table. Wine, tea, hot cocoa for the child, and a bowl of late-winter oranges pass hand to hand.
Elias (the host)
My dear friends—and young Timmy, our newest thinker—tonight let’s play with a fun idea. What if consciousness isn’t something rare that only shows up in complicated brains? What if it’s a lively essence running through everything in the universe—right alongside electrical charges, magnetism, heat, matter, and even time itself—like a modern version of the old ether that scientists once imagined filling space?

To keep it light, we’ll call this essence Pleichyma—from Greek words meaning “more” or “abundant” and “juice” or “fluid.” Picture it as the universe’s abundant, fizzy juice—a playful spark that flows with all the other forces we know. Who wants to start us off?
Dr. Sophia Moreau (philosopher)
I’ll jump in, Elias. The big puzzle for me is how we get vivid experiences—like tasting coffee or seeing red—from stuff that seems totally without feeling. Pleichyma as a fundamental essence, running side-by-side with charge and magnetism, solves that neatly. It’s not something added later; it’s built in from the start, just as basic as gravity or electricity. That way, mind doesn’t have to magically appear—it’s always been part of the mix.
Dr. Mateo Alvarez (physicist)
Sophia, that’s music to my ears! Physics already treats electric charge, magnetic fields, and energy as basic ingredients of reality. Adding Pleichyma as another fundamental “charge”—a kind of awareness-field—feels almost natural. It could ride along with electromagnetic waves, get carried by heat flow, even influence how particles choose paths in quantum events. Think of it like the old ether idea, but instead of just carrying light, this lively ether carries the spark of experience everywhere space and time exist—time itself bending fluidly under intentional nudges.
Robert Charest (humble tinkerer)
If I may chime in humbly, friends—I'm no doctor like the rest of you, but I've tinkered with machines and pondered the stars long enough to have my own out-of-the-box take. What if Pleichyma doesn't just flow evenly? Picture it coalescing—its parts coming together unevenly, never in equal measure unless proximity forces a balance. That's how consciousness emerges into a soul: not a uniform blend, but an asymmetric gathering where parts remain unbound, free to drift, yet deeply interdependent. Like dew drops forming on a leaf—each droplet unique, reliant on the others for shape, but able to evaporate alone. Intention pulls them closer, matter gives them form, time makes the whole thing fluid.
Dr. Lila Chen (biologist)
Robert, your idea lights up my world of cells! They're buzzing with electric currents, magnetic pulls, heat exchanges, and matter reshuffling. If Pleichyma coalesces unevenly like you say—drawn by proximity in crowded membranes or neural clusters—it explains why life feels aware. A single cell's faint spark grows into a soul through interdependent parts: organelles unbound but working together, consciousness flowing as fluidly as nutrients across boundaries.
